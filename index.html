Epsilon & Delta

Main Idea

Epsilon & Delta will hopefully be a series of mathematics workbooks for elementary school children. The goal is to expose children to mathematics (and some computer science) that does not require much previous mathematical knowledge but that is nevertheless generally taught much later in their education. Sets, prime numbers and functions are good examples.

The books have two characters: Epsilon and Delta. The characters have discussions, such as if there is a largest number, and have adventures, such as building towers out of blocks and figuring out how many blocks they need. Each book has a special topic and story, and several sets of exercises. The content is cumulative.

Age

The books are intended for children in first to third grade and will continue with them through school.

Format

The first four books each have twelve pages of content. The books will be mailed each month throughout the school year. They are written by Brendan Farrell and illustrated by Natalie Chupil.

When to Start

The first book will be sent out in September 2022.

Parents

Parents do not need to have any math experience to work with their children on the books. Each workbook will likely take 1-2 hours for a child to work through, ideally spread over several days within a month. It will certainly be helpful if a parent works with the child on the workbooks and discusses the work with the child.

Commitment

If a child sticks with it for several years, she/he should develop a familiarity and hopefully an enjoyment of mathematics. The focus is not helping children with standard school mathematics or preparing them for standardized tests. A child will need to stick with the books long enough to slowly accumulate exposure to the ideas.
